# Journey Map: AI Career Path Copilot

## Discover
Uyen sees classmates sharing internship results and realizes she does not know which tech role fits her best. She searches job boards and social media for fresher tech roles, but the advice is fragmented. A university community post introduces AI Career Path Copilot as a tool that can analyze her CV and GitHub to show her closest role, skill gaps, and next steps.

## Aware
Uyen opens the product page and sees that the tool is designed for Vietnamese CS/IT students, fresh graduates, and junior tech workers. The promise is specific: upload a CV, optionally connect GitHub, choose target roles, and receive explainable role match scores plus a 30-day plan. She is interested because the product connects learning, portfolio, and job readiness instead of only showing courses or job listings.

## Convert
Uyen starts the onboarding flow. She uploads her CV, connects GitHub, and selects target roles such as Frontend Developer, Backend Developer, QA Engineer, Data Analyst, and AI Engineer. Before analysis begins, the product explains what data is used and gives her control over optional inputs. Within one minute, she reaches a personalized dashboard showing her strongest role match, evidence from her profile, and the first skill gap to address.

## Retain
Uyen returns to the dashboard during the next month to follow the 30-day plan. After she completes a suggested mini-project or updates her GitHub, she reruns the assessment and sees her match score improve. The product keeps her engaged by showing practical next actions: what to learn this week, what portfolio project to finish, and which internships or junior jobs are realistic now.

## Advocate
Uyen shares her dashboard and portfolio project plan with a mentor, then recommends the tool to classmates who are also confused about their first role. If she lands interviews or feels more confident applying, she becomes a strong advocate because the product helped her turn vague anxiety into a visible career path.
