# Persona: "Uncertain Uyen"

## Descriptive
*   **Narrative:** Uyen is a final-year Information Technology student in Ho Chi Minh City. She has completed university coursework, a few online certificates, and several small GitHub projects, but she is unsure whether she is closest to Frontend Developer, QA Engineer, Data Analyst, or another junior tech role. She feels pressure to get employed quickly, but every job description seems to require a different stack and more experience than she has.
*   **Demographic:** 22 years old, final-year IT student, based in Ho Chi Minh City, applying for internships and fresher roles.
*   **Psychographic:** Ambitious, anxious, practical, and highly motivated by visible progress. She is comfortable with AI tools, but skeptical of generic advice that does not explain why it applies to her.

## Motivations
*   Wants to choose a realistic first tech role based on her current strengths, not social media hype.
*   Wants a clear 30-day plan that turns scattered learning into employability.
*   Wants to build a portfolio project that recruiters can understand and that matches her target role.
*   Wants confidence before applying, especially when comparing herself with stronger classmates and bootcamp graduates.

## Interactions
*   Uses her laptop for coding, GitHub, CV editing, and online learning.
*   Uses her phone to browse job groups, LinkedIn posts, TikTok career advice, and internship listings.
*   Checks TopCV, LinkedIn, university communities, Facebook groups, and company career pages for junior roles.
*   Has used ChatGPT for explanations and code help, but has not found a tool that connects her skills to real jobs.

## Pain Points
*   Job descriptions are inconsistent and intimidating, making it hard to know which requirements are truly important for entry-level roles.
*   Learning platforms recommend more courses, but do not tell her which skills are blocking her from a specific role.
*   Her GitHub and CV do not clearly communicate her strongest direction.
*   Generic career advice makes her feel more overwhelmed because it ignores her current coursework, projects, and local job market.
*   She worries about sharing personal CV and GitHub data unless the product clearly explains what it uses and why.

## Moments that Matter
*   Uploading her CV and connecting GitHub for the first time.
*   Seeing her top role match score and understanding why that role was recommended.
*   Discovering the highest-priority skill gap that prevents her from applying confidently.
*   Receiving a concrete 30-day learning plan and one portfolio project idea.
*   Deciding whether to apply for a listed internship or keep learning first.
